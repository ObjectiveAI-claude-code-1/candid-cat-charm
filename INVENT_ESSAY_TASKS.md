Task 1 — Evaluate Authenticity of Behavior: Look at the cat in the image and judge whether it is genuinely being itself. Determine if the cat's behavior appears natural, instinctive, and unperformed — the kind of action that would have happened whether or not a camera was present. A cat kneading a blanket with half-closed eyes, wedging itself into a too-small box, or leaping impulsively from a shelf all carry the weight of the involuntary. Contrast this with signs that the moment was engineered: a cat placed in a costume, arranged with props, or coaxed into a position. The task is to feel the difference between a cat that is living and a cat that has been directed, and to score the image higher when the behavior reads as authentic and unselfconscious.

Task 2 — Evaluate Emotional Resonance: Judge whether the image stirs a genuine feeling in the viewer — delight, tenderness, humor, wonder, or quiet contemplation. The task is to determine whether the image moves past being merely a visual record of a cat and becomes something felt. A cat mid-yawn with absurd vulnerability, a kitten curled into an impossibly small ball, or a cat staring out a rain-streaked window with perfect stillness all create an emotional bridge between the cat's experience and the human viewer. Images that are technically competent but emotionally flat should score lower than images that are imperfect but alive with feeling. The task is to measure the charm — that hard-to-name quality that makes a person stop, smile, or feel a pang of affection.

Task 3 — Evaluate Spontaneity of Capture: Assess whether the image feels like a moment seized rather than a moment constructed. Look for the telltale signs of happenstance — slight imperfections in framing, everyday backgrounds, available light, a sense that the photographer grabbed the nearest camera before the moment dissolved. These imperfections are not flaws; they are proof the moment was real and fleeting. Contrast this with images that feel heavily produced — studio-perfect lighting, carefully arranged backgrounds, composition so deliberate it feels sterile — where the apparatus of production inserts itself between the viewer and the moment. A skilled photographer using a good camera does not automatically score low; the question is whether the image feels like discovery or manufacture. Score higher when the image radiates the energy of "I couldn't believe what my cat was doing so I grabbed my phone."
